Two suggestions for RSS 2.0

To: dave@userland.com, f8dy@diveintomark.org

Two silly ideas for the next iteration of the Simple Syndication format. My angle is as someone who (a) has hand-built site-maintenance tools that generate an XML feed, and (b) started building my own RSS 0.9x consumer and discovered none of my XSLT worked with RSS 1.0...

First, allow the container document to have an arbitrary root element, and make the channel wrapper optional as well. Syndication processors should ignore everything apart from what in 0.92 documents are the subelements of channel. The item elements must still follow all the others. The recommended envelope for documents containing syndication data and nothing else remains as in 0.92 (apart from the version number): 

<rss version="2.0" xmlns="http://my.netscape.com/rdf/simple/0.9/">
    <channel>...</channel>
</rss>

For the sake of XML fans, producers should generate the xmlns attribute as shown above. Nevertheless, for compatibility, consumers should recognize syndication elements even if they appear unprefixed, even if in the wrong namespace (or with no namespace).

The intention in removing the requirement for a particular envelope is that in the future syndication data can be embedded in any XML document; in other words, syndication becomes a ‘module’ that can be mixed in with other formats in any way that is convenient.

In particular, 0.9 documents are compatible with 2.0 consumers, because item elements are now permitted to follow the channel rather than be contained within it. (I admit there are vanishingly few version-0.9 feeds left, so you may not care about this!) I believe it also allows syndication data to be embedded in RSS 1.0 documents (the RDF-specific stuff being simply ignored), though I have not checked this thoroughly. Some finessing may be required.

I’m pretty sure a form or words can be thrashed out that describes this slightly looser format without sacrificing the concise style of the existing RSS 0.9x documentation.

Second, I would like to suggest using the name XML Site Syndication (XSS) for this format. At the same time we can start referring to the RDF-based RSS as RDF-SS or RDF/RSS. The reason I want to do this is that—despite having many features in common—the two formats are different things, being the results of separate development processes, and it is confusing for outsiders that they have the same initials.

For what it’s worth, I think XSS is a nice name. The X reminds us it is built on XML, not on top of an RDF layer on top of XML. Or, the X reminds us that the new format is now extensible because XSS documents can exploit modules defined separately (because of the namespace paragraph Dave already added). You can pronounce it ‘excesses’, which gives it an attractively hedonistic flavour :-)

I realize there are politics and history involved here, and that there are good reasons for not changing something’s name (since name changes are also confusing). On the other hand, I think having distinct names will make it easier to discuss differences and similarities between XSS and RDF-SS on a purely technical level.

Anyway, that’s my 2 ideas. It may seem odd that on the one hand I am proposing a technical fix that just happens to make RDF-SS look like a special case of XSS, whereas on the other I am apparently cementing the fork by giving the different branches their own names. But I really think that this will let us get back to the interesting bit (generating and consuming syndication data) and leave the discussion of the ‘ownership’ of the RSS name to future historians...

Bye for now

—Damian
